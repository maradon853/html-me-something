Aries

Chrysomallos, the golden winged ram, was the child of Posidon, who was the brother of Zeus. The ram was eventually sacraficed back to Posidon and became the constellation Aries.

Tarus

At one time, Zeus transformed his mistress, Lo, into the bull, Tarus. He also changed himself into a cow so they could be secret about their affair.

Gemini

Castor, a mortal, and Pollux, a son of Zeus, were twin half brothers. Pollux asked his father to let him share his immortality with Castor, so Zeus transformed them into the constellation Gemini.

Cancer

A crab attacked Heracles, a son of Zeus, while he battled the many headed hydra. Hera, Zeus' wife but Heracles' enemy, placed the crab among the stars as the constellation Cancer.

Leo

Zeus' daughter, Artemis, a hunter, had a handmaiden named Cyrene. Cyrene once battled and dominated a lion using her feminine powers of determination and strength, rather than brute force.

Virgo

Artemis had a twin brother named Apollo, who had a daughter named Parthenos, meaning virgin. She died at a young age and Apollo made the constellation Virgo to commemorate her death.

Libra

The constellation of Libra is based on Themis, who was the child of Gaia and Uranus (earth and sky). She was a titoness, and one of Zeus' lovers. Her ability to forsee the future enabled her to become one of the oracles of delphi, which led her to become the goddess of divine justice.

Scorpio

Artemis once sent a scorpion to battle Orion, who had threatned to hunt and kill all the animals in the world. After the scorpion killed Orion, Zeus placed it in the sky as the constellation Scorpius, and did the same for Orion, to remind mortals to curb excessive pride.

Sagittarius

Some say this constellation is the image of the satyr Crotus, the son of Pan. Crotus is seen as an archer, who's arrow is pointed directly at the heart of Scorpius, possibly to avenge Orion.

Capricorn

The constellation Capricornus is sometimes identified as Pan, but with a fish tail. He once saved himself form the monster, Typhon, by transforming into part fish and swimming away in a river.

Aquarius

Ganymede, the son of the Trojan king, Tros, was taken by Zeus to mount olympius to be a cup carrier to the gods. His vase pours into the constellation of Piscis, the two fish.

Pisces

Aphrodite, a daughter of Zeus, originally came from an egg from the sea. Two fish rescued the egg and brought it to shore. After she was born, she put them into the night sky as a form of gratitude and they became the constellation Pisces.
