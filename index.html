Astrological Mythology

There are many variations of the anchient myths that explain the constellations of the zodiac. Below are some versions of the stories that define our astrological signs.

  • Aries
  • Taurus
  • Gemini
  • Cancer
  • Leo
  • Virgo
  • Libra
  • Scorpio
  • Sagittarius
  • Capricorn
  • Aquarius
  • Pisces

Aries

    Chrysomallos was a special golden winged ram. He was the child of Poseidon, who was Zeus’ brother. Nephele, a cloud nymph, asked the ram to save her children, Phrixus and Helle, who were to be sacrificed by their jealous stepmother, Ino. After rescuing her children, Chrysomallos was sacrificed to Ares, the god of war, and became the constellation Aries.

Taurus

    At one time, Zeus had transformed himself into a beautiful white bull to seduce the princess Europa. They had an affair and children unbeknown to his wife, Hera. Zeus created the constellation Taurus to commemorate his love for Europa, as well as his stubbornness to capture the attention of those he desires.

Gemini

    Castor and Pollux were twins, but also half brothers. Castor was a mortal and Pollux was a demigod, the son of Zeus. After Castor was fatally wounded in battle, Pollux asked his father to let him share his immortality with Castor. Zeus then transformed the twins into the constellation Gemini, granting them both access to Olympius and Hades.

Cancer

    Zeus had many children from adultery, one whom being Heracles. Hera tried many times to destroy Heracles out of spite and jealousy. She sent the crab, Karkinos, to attack Heracles while he battled the many headed hydra. Heracles defeated Karkinos, but Hera placed the crab in the stars as the constellation Cancer out of gratitude for his service.

Leo

    The constellation Leo is often connected to the tale of Heracles defeating the Nemean Lion. But the lion is also connected to the story of Cyrene, the handmaiden of Artemis, who was the huntress daughter of Zeus. Cyrene once battled and dominated a lion using her feminine powers of determination and strength, rather than masculine brute force. Apollo, Artemis’ brother, witnessed Cyrene’s fight with the lion, her victory causing him to fall in love with her.

Virgo

    Apollo once saved the princess Parthenos and her sister Hemithea from a joint suicide attempt. They planned to jump off a cliff to escape their father’s rath. After saving them both, Apollo turned them into goddesses, and eventually turned Parthenos into the constellation Virgo.

Libra

    The constellation of Libra is based on Themis, who was the child of Gaia and Uranus (earth and sky). She was a titoness, and one of Zeus' lovers. Her ability to foresee the future enabled her to become one of the oracles of delphi, which led her to become the goddess of divine justice. This is why her constellation is symbolized by balancing scales.

Scorpio

    Artemis once sent Scorpio, the scorpion, to battle Orion because had threatened to hunt and kill all the animals in the world. After Scorpio killed Orion, Zeus placed it in the sky as the constellation Scorpius, and did the same for Orion. This was done to remind mortals to curb excessive pride.

Sagittarius

    While often depicted as a centaur, some say the constellation of Sagittarius is the image of the satyr Crotus, who was the son of Pan. Crotus is seen as an archer, who's arrow is pointed directly at the heart of Scorpius, possibly to avenge Orion.

Capricorn

    The constellation Capricornus can be identified as Pan, the satyr god of the wild. But he is depicted with a fish tail instead of goat legs. This is because Pan once saved himself from the monster, Typhon, by transforming into part fish and swimming away in a river.

Aquarius

    The constellation Aquarius is based on Ganymede, the son of the Trojan king, Tros. He was a handsome water bearer who was taken by Zeus to mount olympus to be a cup carrier to the gods. His vase pours into the constellation of Piscis, the two fish.

Pisces

    Aphrodite, one of Zeus’ daughters, originally came from an egg that was in the ocean. Two fish rescued the egg and brought it to shore. After she was born, she put the fish into the night sky as a form of gratitude and they became the constellation Pisces.
